About Desk Health:

Purpose:

Many people spend a lot of the day sitting, such as students sitting and studying for hours, and people working at a desk job sitting for most of the day. This can lead to a decrease in overall health and well-being. Desk Health was created to help encourage people such as these, to follow some healthy habits that can help combat the negative effects of spending too much time sitting.

Desk Health is designed to help many aspects of health and well-being such as cardiovascular fitness, muscular fitness, eye health, and mental well-being.

How this App Can Help You:

This app can help provide reminders and motivation to keep feeling your best while still doing your work! Desk Health can help remind you to rotate between sitting and standing every 30 minutes to decrease the amount of time you spend sitting, and it can help remind you to follow the 20-20-20 rule for eye health (every 20 minutes look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds). It can also remind you to take a break from work every now and then for mental well being, if you so chose. Feel free to change time intervals for standing, sitting looking at something farther away, or anything else with timers you can customize, create, and delete!

You can alsoset long-term and/or short-term goals and track your progress with Desk Health. Along with tracking goals, you can log workouts and track exercise progress. Want to lose weight or gain weight? Want to get better at drinking water? Desk Health can help motivate you!

Desk health is meant to help motivate you to stay healthy both at and away from the desk to keep up with overall health and well-being!

Why Download:

Work Mode Can Help You Stand More to Be Healthier

Many people spend a lot of the day sitting, such as students sitting and studying for hours, and people working at a desk job sitting for most of the day. This can lead to a decrease in overall health and well-being. Desk Health was created to help encourage people such as these, to follow some healthy habits that can help combat the negative effects of spending too much time sitting.

These negative factors associated with sedentary time are greater risk for all-cause mortality, cardiovascular disease (CVD), cancer, and Type 2 diabetes millitus (T2DM) in adults. However, studies show that alternating between sitting and standing can lead to reduced daily sedentary time, improved lower extremity vascular function, and improved markers of cardiometabolic health (Bodker et al., 2021). It has been found that standing for at least 30 minutes every hour (no more than 45 minutes) can lead to health benefits of being less sedentary (Callaghan et al., 2015). The Work Mode feature on Desk Health can help remind and motivate you to follow these recommendations, rotating between sitting and standing!

Exercise Mode Can Help You Get the Exercise You Need

Reducing time sitting everyday is great for health, but it is no substitute for staying physically active with exercise. Even for people who spend lots of time sedentary, if they meet recommended physical activity levels, they have a 30% lower risk for all-cause mortality (ACSM, 2018). This not only elucidates the importance of physical activity, but it also demonstrates how vital it is to avoid sedentary behaviors. A 30% reduction of risk for all-cause mortality is good, but not great. That is why exercise should be paired with a less sedentary lifestyle to reduce sedentary-related risks as much as possible. There are so many benefits of regular physical activity, and the American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM) (2018) lists some of these out:

  • Increased VO2 max
  • Decreased amount of air entering lungs per minute at a given submaximal intensity
  • Decreased amount of oxygen the heart needs to function at a given submaximal intensity
  • Decreased heart rate and blood pressure at a given submaximal intensity
  • Increased capillary density in skeletal muscle
  • Increased exercise threshold for the accumulation of lactate in the blood
  • Increased exercise threshold for the onset of disease signs or symptoms
  • Reduced resting systolic/ diastolic blood pressure
  • Increased serum high-density lipoprotien (HDL) cholesterol and decreased serum triglycerides
  • Reduced total body fat, reduced intra-abdominal fat
  • Reduced insulin needs, improved glucose tolerance
  • Reduced blood platelet adhesiveness and aggregation
  • Reduced inflammation
  • Higher activity and/ or fitness levels are associated with lower death rates from coronary artery disease (CAD)
  • Higher activity and/ or fitness levels are associted with lower incidence rates for cardiovascular disease (CVD), CAD, stroke, T2DM, metabolic syndrome, osteoperotic fractures, cancer of the colon and breast, and gallbladder disease
  • Cardiovascular and all-cause mortality are reduced in people who previously had a heart attack who participate in cardiac rehabilitation
  • Decreased anxiety and depression
  • Improved cognitive function
  • Enhanced physical function and independent living in older individuals
  • Enhanced feelings of well-being
  • Enhanced performance of work, recreational, and sport activities
  • Reduced risk of falls and injuries from falls in older individuals
  • Prevention or reduction of functional limitations in older adults
  • Effective therapy for many chronic diseases in older adults

Desk Health's Exercise Mode can help motivate you to keep up with your exercise, while you record your workouts and track your progress! Here are general exercise guidelines that ACSM (2018) provides for healthy adults:

Cardiorespiratory

  • Moderate intensity exercise for 30-60 minutes at least 5 days per week OR
  • Vigorous intensity exercise for 20-60 minutes at least 3 days per week OR
  • A combinatioon of 3-5 days of moderate and vigorous intensity exercise per week
  • These recommended amount of exercise in one day can be met with one session, or in multiple sessions lasting at least 10 minutes.

Strength

  • Resistance exercise for each major muscle group 2-3 days per week of with at least 48 hours between resistance workouts for same muscle groups
  • Resistance training for each muscle group for 2-4 sets with 8-12 repetitions per set and a 2-3 minute rest between sets
  • People over 65 years of age and/or deconditioned individuals should start with at least 1 set of 10-15 repetitions of very light to light intensity resistance exercise.
  • Once people adapt to their resistance workout routine, they should gradually increase resistance, number of sets, or frequency of training.

Flexibility

  • Stretch each major muscle-tendon unit (after warming muscles up) at least 2-3 days per week, every day being most effective.
  • Hold each stretch at the point where there is a tight feeling, or slight discomfort, for 10-30 seconds
  • Repeat each stretch for 2-4 times for best results.

Weight Tracker/ BMI Calculator Can Help You Monitor Your Progress to Gain, Lose or Maintain Your Weight

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a calculation of someone's weight (in killograms) divided by their height (in meters) squared. People with BMIs that are too high or too low are more likely to have adverse health conditions. Using Desk Health's BMI calculator, you can calculate your BMI without having to do the math yourself by simply entering your height and weight. Here is ACSM's (2018) BMI classification chart:

Goal Tracker Can Help Keep You Motivated to Reach Your Goals

One of the best ways to make progress with anything is to set short-term and long-term goals that are specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-specific (SMART). Setting goals like this allows progress to be tracked, and can add satisfaction of completing goals. This can then lead to increased motivation. Use Desk Health's Goal Tracker feature to create your own goals and track your progress with them!

Reflection Journal Can Help You Reflect on Your Progress in a Fun and Relaxing Way

Writing in a journal can be a fun and relaxing way to think about any goals and progress that has been made. It can be a great motivational tool to think and write about what you'd like to accomplish, and it can boost happiness and well-being to reflect on your accomplishments, or people and things you are grateful for in life. Journaling can be a great self-love tool, self-discovery tool and a way to work on self-improvement. Desk Health's Reflection Journal gives you a way to write and reflect, making it easy to reflect on goal progress or to write about anything you want!

Hydration Log Can Help You Make Sure to Get Enough Water Everyday!

Making sure to stay hydrated is vital for people to feel and look their best. Consuming enough water can improve cognitive performance and mood; decrease risk for delirium; improve gastrointestinal function; protect kidneys and their function; help regulate blood volume, blood pressure, and heart rate; help with orthostatic hypotension; possibly prevent dehydration-related headaches; improve skin thickness, density, offset water loss through skin, and improve skin hydration; and possibly reduce risk for a variety of chronic diseases (Popkin et al., 2011). Use Desk Health's Hydration Log to keep track of your water intake to stay healthy!
